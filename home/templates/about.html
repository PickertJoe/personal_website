Who Am I and Why Did I Make a Website?

So, why did I create this website? In short, I've fallen head over heels for computer programming. As a result, I decided to develop this website from scratch to expand my technical skillset, share a few of my projects in software engineering, and elevate my digital profile.

Although my educational background is primarily in civil/environmental engineering, I've been slowly but steadily building my knowledge base in programming through a mixture of formal classes and rigorous self-study. My first taste of programming came through an introductory computer science course taught in C++. Although I found the language difficult to learn at first, I fell in love with the problem solving and abstract thinking that software engineering demands.

The final project for the course involved creating a library database, which you can find in my project portfolio. All other code I wrote for this course can also be found on my Github. I'd highly recommend checking both out!

At the same time, I was also introduced to data science through an undergraduate research project. Since the fall fo 2017, I've been working with Dr. Pam Sullivan here at the University of Kansas to explore the long term ramifications of nitrate contamination in groundwater. Part of this project involved pulling in 1.4 million data points representing nearly two years of groundwater data from nearby wells to contextualize the movement of water (and hence, nitrate) through the subsurface.

This project has given me invaluable experience in the manipulation and visualization of vast quantities of data. And while I've worked primarily in R throughout this project, I've found that many of the data manipulation skills I've acquired have carried over into my work in Python.

A few of the figures I've created through my research can be found in my project portfolio, and all of my source code in R is of course available on my GitHub. The ultimate goal of this study is to be published in a major scientific journal by May of 2020. Stay tuned for future updates!

Although software engineering has become a major passion of mine, my interests expand far beyond the computer screen! Before going back to school, I spent several years working as a mountain guide in Colorado. While the job was stressful and ever-changing, it marked the fulfillment of a lifelong aspiration of mine. After starting out as a complete novice in several of the activities I had to guide, I worked my way up in two years to become the Head Mountain Guide and Climbing Director of Rocky Mountain High Adventure Base.

During my time in management at the Base, I had the immense priviledge of training and growing close to the nine other guides working on my staff. Having the opportunity to teach and lead them as they grew into guides in their own right was an incredibly fulfilling experience, and one that I would like to experience later in my career.

And while I may have left my professional guiding days behind, I've carried many of the hobbies and skills that I cultivated there into my current life. To this day, I remain an avid mountain biker and road cyclist, and you can be sure to find me in the mountains whenever a vacation opportunity arises.

Working as a mountain guide also endowed me with a wealth of customer service skills, with one of the most salient being anticipating needs of my clients before they arose. When guiding others in the backcountry, you are responsible not only for the safety of your clients but also for ensuring that they enjoy their trip. With backpacking treks as long as five days, I frequently became close to the people I was guiding. This allowed me to learn much about their individual personalities, and pick up on subtle cues that might indicate the need for a break or a game to pass the time.

When I'm not on the road or on a trail, I also enjoy playing music from time to time. I started playing the violin when I was 9 years old, and played in my school orchestra all the way through graduation. Since then, I've remained active in a few smaller community orchestras, and have always relished the diverse range of new people that music has allowed me to meet.

If the weather's poor, or I'm simply too tired to do anything else, one of my favorite ways to unwind after a long day is playing video games. My favorite of all time is probably the MMROPG Old School Runescape. Much like this website, my character in the game is the result of small, consistent increments of progress. Believe it or not, it's actually a revival of a past version of the game I played growing up. Somethings never change, you know?

Aside from Runescape, I also enjoy playing grand strategy games such as anything in the Total War series or some of the historically specific titles made by Paradox Interactive (e.g. Hearts of Iron, Europa Universalis, etc.).

I'm also a voracious reader, and read anywhere from 20-40 books per year depending on their lengths. My favorite genre has always been historical fiction, though the high fantasy of J.R.R. Tolkien and George R.R. Martin have always held a special place in my heart. My favorite author is Ernest Hemmingway, primarily because of his masterpiece For Whom the Bell Tolls. Fyodor Dostoevsky and Leo Tolstoy come in tied for a close second though, as I've always been partial to classical Russian literature.

I'm quite active over on Goodreads.com, and try to give thoughtful reviews for each book that I finish. I've always found the community over there to be very engaging and friendly, and would highly recommend making an account if you're an avid reader or are looking for book suggestions. If you do, definitely be sure to add me as a friend!

So that, in a nutshell, is who I am. While the projects on my GitHub or the bullet points on my resume might convey my specific, technical skills and expertise, I felt it was important to share the breadth of my experience. In a world of increasing hyper-specialization, I take immense pride in being a generalist in every sense of the word. Though I may be a relative newcomer to the world of software engineering, my greatest strength is my ability to learn rapidly, independently, and dynamically.

And I have no doubt that I could make a difference for you.

If you'd like to know more, please don't hesitate to contact me.
